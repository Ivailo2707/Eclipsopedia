Lunar Eclipses

What is a Lunar Eclipse?

A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the Moon's surface. During this celestial event, the Moon can take on various shades, from a subtle gray to a deep reddish hue.

Types of Lunar Eclipses

Lunar eclipses can be total, partial, or penumbral. A total lunar eclipse happens when the Earth completely blocks the Sun's light from reaching the Moon, resulting in a striking red Moon. Partial lunar eclipses occur when only a portion of the Moon is in Earth's shadow, while penumbral eclipses are more subtle and involve only the outer shadow of the Earth.
